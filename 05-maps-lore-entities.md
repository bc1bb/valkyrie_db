## 1. Story / Lore Background — The Clone-Pilot Premise

The core conceit: **the Valkyrie are immortal cloned ace pilots.** A renegade uses **illegal cloning/consciousness-transfer technology** to capture the consciousness (and recovered DNA) of dying/fallen fighter pilots at the moment of death and insert them into purpose-built clone bodies. The pilots awaken with fragmented memories of their previous deaths and failures. (PlayStation Blog; "What's Your Story, Pilot?")

- The man behind the technology is **Jirai "Fatal" Laitanen**, who was building a technologically advanced army of clone pilots — the **Schism** — and presented himself to recruits as rescuer, redeemer and mentor.
- **Rán Kavik** was among the first pilots subjected to this process. She later discovered the truth: rather than being *rescued*, she had been **deliberately targeted and murdered** so that her talents as a pilot could be cloned and exploited to serve the Schism's ambitions.
- On learning this, Rán **broke away from the Schism** and founded her own fiercely independent fighter force, the **Valkyrie**, dedicated to making their own mark and to taking revenge on Fatal.
- The single-player content (**Chronicles** / "Recall Missions" — reported as 4 missions) frames the player as a newly-awakened clone piecing together how they died and were recruited. When the player-pilot dies, consciousness is downloaded into a new clone body ("Clone Vat").

## 4. Maps / Multiplayer Arenas / Environments

The game shipped (2016) with a core set of arenas and added more through free updates, culminating in the Warzone expansion (2017) which advertised **9 custom-crafted multiplayer combat arenas** total ("from the snowy mountains of Solitude to the scattered wreckage of Necropolis").

### Launch / early maps

| Map | Environment / Description | Location & Lore |
|---|---|---|
| **Necropolis** | A drifting **graveyard of destroyed ships / wreckage**; debris fields used for cover | Graveyard of ships destroyed at the **Battle of Esmes III** (named battle) |
| **Shipyard** | Built from intricate tunnels and beams of a ship-construction facility | A **former Caldari facility** later **assimilated by the Guristas pirate clan**; the supercarrier **Phoenix** (>3,000 m) is docked here *(see ships)* |
| **Cathedral** | Large central structure used for **religious gatherings**; cavernous central hall | Religious/spiritual installation (Amarr-flavored religious theme implied) |
| **Forge** | Asteroid/industrial environment (ship-construction "forge" theme) | One of the original five maps |
| **Convoy** | Environment built around ships/convoy traffic | Listed among the original ~5 maps (preview-era). *Lower confidence — older preview source; may have been renamed/cut* |

### Maps added via free updates

| Map | Update | Environment / Description | Location & Lore |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Crossroads** | Carrier Assault update (~June 2016) | Multiplayer map supporting Scout & Survival modes | Home of **Station 27** (~10 km long), located "at the Crossroads" |
| **Gateway** | "Gatecrash" update (Dec 2016) | An **"iceteroid" field** around a **massive, unfinished warp/jump gate** in a polar-toned blue-white nebula; central cylindrical "core" with warp gates at each end (boost gate, escape routes) | Warp gate based on **Gallente** gate architecture (ring structure); under construction |
| **Solitude** | (icy biome map) | A **snowy, rocky planetary landscape** with the **Solitude base** in the distance; exit from a carrier onto terrain | Described as one of the largest, most challenging maps due to scale and terrain (named star system **Solitude** is a real EVE region) |

### Warzone expansion maps (2017)

| Map | Environment / Description | Location & Lore |
|---|---|---|
| **Fleet** | A **massive Sisters of EVE space station** "preparing for war"; bright, busy, with large SoE ships (Stratios, Nestor) creating fly-through routes/cover; big ships won't yield to fighters | Sisters of EVE station |
| **Outpost** | A **dark, foreboding, fog-shrouded, lightning-dappled circular Drifter base**; narrow internal tunnels around the rim; Drifter battleships docked inside | A remote **Drifter** structure "that holds the key to new technologies" |

### Other map-related content

- **Wormholes** — Warzone added rotating **weekly Wormhole** variants that twist the rules/gameplay each week (not a fixed arena).
- **Carrier Assault mode** is played on existing maps (e.g., Solitude) and centers on attacking an enemy **carrier's Core** via a trench-run access tunnel (Skirmish → Attack → Breach stages).

**Map roster summary (named arenas):** Necropolis, Shipyard, Cathedral, Forge, Convoy *(uncertain)*, Crossroads, Gateway, Solitude, Fleet, Outpost.

## 8. Cross-Reference: Fatal & the Guristas (EVE Online canon)

The antagonist **Jirai "Fatal" Laitanen** is a deep-cut EVE Online lore character (the game ties its villain to existing canon):

- A shrewd, charismatic, vain and greedy captain of the **Caldari Navy 37th ("Octopus") Squadron**.
- Passed over for promotion; in the same week his friend **Korako Kosakami** ("**The Rabbit**" — introverted technical genius) was blamed for a lethal crash landing.
- The pair **deserted**, stealing two **Condor-class frigates**, fleeing to pirate-infested space between Caldari and Gallente territory.
- They founded the **Guristas Pirates** (name = amalgam of two Caldari words meaning "naughty people" / slang for "gang"), based in the **Venal** region.
- Famous for kidnapping the Gallente Federation ambassador to the Caldari State, **Luecin Rileau**.
- Canon chronicle: **"Fatal and the Rabbit."**

> Note: spelling varies across sources — **"Kosakami"** (EVE Universe/lore wikis) vs **"Koskarami"/"Koskami"** (some EVE: Valkyrie press). Canonical EVE spelling is **Korako Kosakami**.

Sources: [Jirai Laitanen – EVE Universe](https://universe.eveonline.com/lore/jirai-laitanen); [Fatal and the Rabbit – EVE Universe Chronicle](https://universe.eveonline.com/chronicles/fatal-and-the-rabbit); [Korako Kosakami – Backstage Lore Wiki](https://wiki.eve-inspiracy.com/index.php?title=Korako_Kosakami); [Guristas Pirates – EVE University](https://wiki.eveuniversity.org/Guristas_Pirates)
