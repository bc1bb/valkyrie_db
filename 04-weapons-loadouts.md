# EVE: Valkyrie – Warzone — Weapons, Modules, Loadouts & Upgrades

**Game:** EVE: Valkyrie – Warzone (CCP Games / CCP Newcastle). Originally launched 2016 (VR-only, "EVE: Valkyrie"); the free *Warzone* expansion (Sept 2017) added flatscreen/non-VR play, the Covert class, and reworked the fleet. Servers were shut down **5 August 2022**, so the official site (`evevalkyrie.com`, `support.evevalkyrie.com`) is largely offline — much data below comes from archived guides, press articles, and Steam community guides.

> **Scope note:** Warzone fields **13 ships across 4 classes** (Assault, Heavy, Support, Covert), each with a fixed weapon kit: a **Primary**, a **Secondary**, a **Tertiary** (ability/module), and an **Ultra** super-ability. Loadouts are largely *per-ship hull*, not freely mix-and-match — you tune a hull via its **upgrade tree** rather than swapping weapons. Some ship names below (e.g. Spectre, Maelstrom) trace to the *original* 2016 game's Heavy line and were partly superseded by the Warzone roster; these are flagged as **[uncertain / legacy]**.

---

## 1. Loadout Structure (every ship)

Each hull carries four slots:

| Slot | Role |
|------|------|
| **Primary** | Main gun (cannon / laser / beam) — unlimited ammo, usually heat- or cooldown-gated. |
| **Secondary** | A second offensive or support weapon (missiles, drain/repair beam, alt-fire). |
| **Tertiary** | A defensive/utility ability or "module" (countermeasures, boost, self-repair, sonar, etc.). |
| **Ultra** | A charged super-ability built up over the match (super-missile, invulnerability, AoE stun, nova bomb, team buff). |

Sources: Steam Assault/Heavy/Support guides; PCGamesN class guide.

## 2. ASSAULT Class (formerly "Fighter" / "Wraith" class)

General-purpose damage dealers; nimble, medium-range. 4 ships.

| Ship | Primary | Secondary | Tertiary (module/ability) | Ultra |
|------|---------|-----------|---------------------------|-------|
| **Wraith Mk6** | **Assault Cannons** — rapid-fire projectile, great medium-range DPS, **overheats** with sustained fire | **Missile Launcher** — up to **5** missiles, splash damage | **ECM** — deploys chaff to block incoming missiles | **Mjolnir** — one-shot "super missile", catastrophic damage |
| **Jackal** | **Ripper** — high-damage close-range burst shots **with** splash | **Alternate Ripper** — high-damage projectile **without** splash | **Overshield** — adds a shield / repairs damaged shields | **Matter Injection** — unlimited boost for a short duration |
| **Strix** | **Pulse Laser** — high-damage, long-range "sniper" laser | **Missile Pod** — up to **7** missiles, splash | **Dash** — blasts ship forward in a straight line (escape) | **Remove Plasma Vent** — fire unlimited shots with no overheat penalty |
| **Yurei** | **Pulse Cannon** — mid-range automatic pulse cannon, high-speed kills | **Trine Missiles** — up to **3** missiles, splash | **ECM** — chaff, blocks incoming missiles | **Mayhem** — temporary increased weapons damage |

**Wraith Mk6 max stats (representative):** Firepower 7/10, Armour 4/10, Shield 4/10, Speed 5/10 (EVE Valkyrie "Wraith" ship page, via search). Wraith is the tutorial/beginner hull.

## 3. HEAVY Class

Tank/brawler class — heavy armour, slow, devastating up close. 4 ships (Warzone). Primaries are often **head-tracked** (aim follows headset gaze in VR).

| Ship | Primary | Secondary | Tertiary (module/ability) | Ultra |
|------|---------|-----------|---------------------------|-------|
| **Scarab** | **Flak Cannon** — high-damage flak rounds with splash, close range | **Micro Warp Drive (MWD)** — burst speed for escape/repositioning | **EMS** — blocks incoming missiles; converts your projectiles into **EMP rounds** | **Void Storm** — EMP bomb, stuns nearby enemy ships in radius |
| **Vulcan** | **Laser Gatling** — 4 rotating barrels, rapid-fire pulse beam; **accuracy degrades** with sustained fire | **Micro Warp Drive (MWD)** | **Self Repair** — restores armour & shields when safe | **Cocoon** — temporary full invulnerability ("nothing can touch you") |
| **Corvus** | **Tri-Flak** — high close-range damage + extra splash | **Sonar** — reveals cloaked (Covert) enemy ships | **EMS** — missile defence; converts projectiles to EMP | **Silence** — instantly stuns ships in pulse radius |
| **Hydra** | **Flak Cannon** — close-range high-damage splash | **Rail Cannon** — long-range, armour-piercing projectile (punches through armour & shields) | **ECM** — chaff cloud missile defence | **Spectre's Bond** — brief damage immunity |

Sources: Steam "Heavy Ship Guide" (id=1134266763); EVE Valkyrie "Lowdown on the Heavies"; PCGamesN.

### Legacy / original-2016 Heavy ships **[uncertain — may be superseded or renamed in Warzone]**
- **Spectre** — described as the *base* Heavy hull: **Head-Tracked Flak Cannon** (proximity-detonating AoE rounds), **Micro Warp Drive**, **EMS**; armour + shield defence. (Search snippets; original game.)
- **Maelstrom** — Heavy with a special **Mortar**: very slow velocity, very low rate of fire (~1 shell / 2s, longer if firing from only one side), **extreme** direct-hit damage, **proximity detonation** producing a cluster of secondary AoE explosions. Mortar shells fire from the lower surface of the sideways "V" reticle on alternating sides. Often paired with **MWD + EMS**. (Search snippets; original game Heavy.)

> These two are flagged uncertain: sources reference them as original-game Heavies. The Warzone Steam guide lists only Scarab/Vulcan/Corvus/Hydra. They may be the same hulls renamed, or removed.

## 4. SUPPORT Class

Most nimble class; advanced electronics to buff/heal allies or suppress enemies. 4 ships. Carries the game's healing/EW kit.

| Ship | Primary | Secondary | Tertiary (module/ability) | Ultra |
|------|---------|-----------|---------------------------|-------|
| **Banshee** | **Phaser** — mid-range beam, continuous damage | **Drain Beam** — lock-on beam that **drains** enemy shields | **Spiderbots** — robotic drones that heal friendlies or damage enemies | **Fate's Call** — pulse buff: shield recharge to all nearby teammates |
| **Nagi** | **Twin Linked Carbine** — double-barrelled rapid-fire projectiles | **Repair Beam** — recharges allies' shields & armour (friendly counterpart to the drain beam) | **Self Repair** — recharge own shield / repair own armour when safe | **Radiant Burst** — pulse recharges all friendly hulls within radius |
| **Medusa** | **Pulse Cannon** — automatic mid-range pulse cannon | **Apply Mini Overshield** — repair a teammate's shield or add an extra one | **EMS** — blocks incoming missiles; turns pulse cannon rounds into **EMP** | **Death's Grasp** — strips all nearby enemy shields, leaving them vulnerable |
| **Kirin** | **Shard Rifle** — high-spread weapon firing sticky projectiles that detonate over time | **Capacitor Restore Beam** — lock-on beam restoring a teammate's capacitor | **HUD Scrambler** — disorienting pulse that scrambles enemy HUDs | **Dragon's Thorn** — fires a sticky bomb that attaches to an enemy then detonates |

Sources: Steam "Support Ship Guide" (id=1138771785).

## 5. COVERT Class (new in Warzone, Sept 2017)

Stealth "glass cannon" assassin. Light armour/shields. **1 ship.**

| Ship | Primary | Secondary | Signature ability (cloak) | Ultra |
|------|---------|-----------|---------------------------|-------|
| **Shadow** | **Plasma Beam** — hitscan; fires a burst of **4** high-energy shots, or **charge** for a few seconds to release one heavy blast (no lead-reticle needed) | **Mark Target** — marks an enemy for ~10s; marked target takes **increased damage**; long recharge | **Cloak** — invisible for **30s**, then ~**15s** recharge. Firing/marking instantly drops cloak; nearby explosions or flak splash make the ship shimmer; direct hits can disable it | **Violent Whisper** — nova bomb with an "absolutely massive" blast radius (the most destructive Ultra in the game) |

Counter: the Heavy **Corvus's Sonar** (and a "radar ping") can detect cloaked Shadows and put them on the team map.

Sources: PCGamesN "Shadow covert class"; PC Gamer; EVE Valkyrie "Revealed at last: the Covert class".

## 6. Weapon / Ability Taxonomy (cross-class summary)

**Primary weapons seen:** Assault Cannons, Ripper (burst), Pulse Laser, Pulse Cannon, Flak Cannon, Tri-Flak, Laser Gatling, Phaser (beam), Twin Linked Carbine, Shard Rifle, Plasma Beam. (Original game also: Gatling guns / head-tracked variants.)

**Secondary weapons seen:** Missile Launcher (5), Missile Pod (7), Trine Missiles (3), Alternate Ripper, Rail Cannon, Drain Beam, Repair Beam, Apply Mini Overshield, Capacitor Restore Beam, Micro Warp Drive (used as a secondary on some Heavies), Sonar, Mark Target.

**Tertiary modules/abilities seen:**
- *Defensive:* ECM (chaff), EMS (missile block + EMP conversion), Overshield, Self Repair, Cocoon (Ultra-tier on Vulcan).
- *Utility/mobility:* Micro Warp Drive, Dash, Sonar, HUD Scrambler, Spiderbots.

**Ultra abilities (super-weapons) by class:**
- *Assault:* Mjolnir (super-missile), Matter Injection (infinite boost), Remove Plasma Vent (no-overheat), Mayhem (damage buff).
- *Heavy:* Void Storm (EMP stun), Cocoon (invuln), Silence (stun), Spectre's Bond (damage immunity).
- *Support:* Fate's Call (team shield), Radiant Burst (team hull recharge), Death's Grasp (strip enemy shields), Dragon's Thorn (sticky bomb).
- *Covert:* Violent Whisper (nova bomb).

**Status effects:** Primary weapons can apply **EMP** to enemy ships for a limited time (esp. via EMS-converted rounds).

## 7. Progression & Upgrade System

### 7.1 Upgrade trees
- Each ship has its **own branching upgrade tree** of **15 upgrades** to unlock.
- Upgrades fall into **four classes:** **Attack, Defense, Tech, Mobility**.
- You may have **one active upgrade per class** (so up to 4 active at once). Unlocking ≠ activating — you must manually **activate** an unlocked upgrade.
- Most upgrades are **trade-offs** (boost one stat, reduce another); a few **straight buffs** appear deeper in the tree. By design, **no single optimal build** — it's tactical variety, not raw power. CCP stated "there are no end-game ships that give a significant advantage."

### 7.2 XP, cost, and ranks
- Upgrades start at **~1,000 XP each** and get **more expensive** in deeper tiers.
- **XP is earned per-ship** (and per-class). Once a ship's tree is fully unlocked, leftover XP can be moved via an **XP transfer** option to another ship.
- Earning class XP **unlocks new ships and upgrades to craft**.

### 7.3 Crafting currencies & salvage
Crafting ships / upgrades requires two resources:
- **Silver** — in-game currency awarded after each battle.
- **Salvage** — materials collected from destroyed enemies in battle; **team salvage is shared** among all players on the team after a match.

Salvage has **three rarity tiers:**
| Tier | Rarity |
|------|--------|
| **Raw** | Common |
| **Component** | Rare |
| **Prime** | Epic |

Higher-tier ships/upgrades require higher-value salvage. A **Quartermaster** builds upgrades and offers cosmetic options.

### 7.4 Launch tubes
Players start with **1 launch tube** and unlock up to **4 total**; each tube can hold a different ship/variant, letting you switch hulls mid-battle on respawn.

Sources: EVE Valkyrie "How to upgrade your ships in Warzone"; PlayStation.Blog "Everything You Need to Know"; support.evevalkyrie.com Ship Progression (via search snippets); imperium.news review/guide.

## 8. Manufacturers / Brands
**No distinct in-game weapon manufacturer/brand system was found for EVE: Valkyrie.** Weapon and ship names are bespoke (Mjolnir, Ripper, Phaser, etc.) and ship classes evoke Norse/mythological naming (Wraith, Banshee, Medusa, Kirin, Yurei). The EVE *Online* weapon manufacturers (Aliastra, Carthum Conglomerate, etc.) are **lore from the parent franchise, not Valkyrie loadout brands** — do not conflate. **[Marked: could not confirm any Valkyrie-specific brand layer.]**
