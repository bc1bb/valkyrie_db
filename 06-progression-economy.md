# EVE: Valkyrie – Warzone — Progression, Ranks, Economy, Cosmetics & Meta-Progression

**Game:** EVE: Valkyrie – Warzone (CCP Games). Original *EVE: Valkyrie* launched 2016 (Oculus Rift, PSVR, HTC Vive); the free **Warzone** expansion launched **September 26, 2017**, adding PC/PS4 flatscreen (no-VR) support, new ships, maps, modes, and a reworked economy.
**Servers:** CCP shut down EVE: Valkyrie online servers on **August 5, 2022** (the official `evevalkyrie.com` live site is now largely offline; much data below comes from an Azure pre-production mirror and third-party archives).

> **Sourcing note:** The official site (`www.evevalkyrie.com`, `support.evevalkyrie.com`, `content.evevalkyrie.com`) is dead. Many official articles were retrieved via the surviving mirror `evevalkyriesecure-production-pre-production.azurewebsites.net`. Trophy data is from PSNProfiles/MetaGame/PlayStationTrophies. Items marked **[UNCERTAIN]** could not be fully confirmed.

## 1. XP / Leveling System & Pilot Ranks

EVE: Valkyrie – Warzone uses a **two-layered progression** model:

1. **Class XP (per ship / per class)** — earned every match for the ship class you fly. Used to unlock that ship's upgrade tree.
2. **Pilot Reputation → Pilot Rank** — at the end of a match, all Class XP earned feeds into your overall **Pilot Reputation**, which raises your **Rank**. Pilot Reputation has its own milestones, separate from the per-ship class trees.

### How XP is earned
You gain XP simply by playing, plus bonuses for: kills, assists, capturing control points, destroying Carrier nodes and the Carrier core, delivering the relic (Extraction/Carrier modes), healing/supporting teammates, and completing objectives. Some of the largest payouts come from control-point captures, Carrier core/node destruction, and relic delivery.

### Ranks / Level cap
| Attribute | Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Starting rank | 1 | New players begin in Flight School, then Proving Grounds (bots/novices) until ~level 5. |
| Original rank cap | 50 | Pre-Warzone / earlier cap. |
| **Warzone rank cap** | **60** | Cap raised to 60 in Warzone. |
| Heavy class unlock | Rank 3 | Heavy-class daily challenges/ships gated until rank 3. |
| Trophy milestones | Rank 10, 25, 50 | "Graduation Day" (10), "Half A Legend" (25), "Master And Commander" (50). |

- **Total XP to reach Rank 50:** ~**739,000 XP** (via any Multiplayer matchmaking except Custom matches). *(Source: PSN trophy guide.)*
- You only need Rank 50 (not 60) for trophies.

### XP Boosters (Implants)
- **Implants** give a **+50% XP boost** on every completed battle while active.
- Durations available: **1 day, 1 week (7 days), 30 days, 90 days, 180 days.**
- A bonus **7-day XP boost** was granted for linking a CCP Account.
- In the original game, implants cost Gold/Silver; **in Warzone all implants were priced in Silver (Visk)** and can also drop from Reward Capsules.

## 2. Ship Classes & Per-Ship Upgrade Trees ("Skill Tree")

### Ship classes
Sources conflict slightly on class names (the original game and Warzone marketing use different labels):
- **Fighter / Assault** — agile dogfighters (marketing compared the four Assault ships to assault rifle / shotgun / sniper / SMG playstyles).
- **Heavy** — durable, heavy-hitting.
- **Support** — healing/utility (e.g., repair allies).
- **Covert** — stealth class. **[UNCERTAIN]** — listed in the "first hour" Warzone article ("Assault, Heavy, Support, and Covert"), but other official text references only Fighter/Heavy/Support. Warzone reportedly shipped with **13 ships total**, all unlocked from purchase.

> **Original game vs Warzone — ship unlocks:** In the *original* game, ships were unlocked progressively (first Heavy/Support unlocked by playing as Fighter; further ships unlocked by earning XP in that category). In **Warzone, ALL ships are unlocked from the start** — progression shifted entirely to per-ship upgrades + cosmetics.

### Upgrade tree (per ship)
- Each ship has its **own branching upgrade tree of 15 upgrades**.
- Upgrades cost **Class XP**, starting at **1,000 XP per upgrade** and getting more expensive per tier.
- **Four upgrade/mod classes:** **Attack, Defense, Tech, Mobility.**
- You can equip **one active modification per class** (i.e., up to 4 mods equipped at once → trophy "Tactical Mind" = assign 4 mods to a ship).
- **Trade-off design:** Lower-tier mods buff one stat while reducing another; higher-tier upgrades give smaller boosts but remove the trade-offs.
- **XP Transfer service** (via the Quartermaster): convert surplus XP from a maxed-out ship/class into another ship.

> **Crafting:** Warzone simplified crafting to "earn XP per ship → use XP to craft/unlock a Modification for that ship." No separate material-crafting economy for mods (salvage is consumed differently — see below).

## 3. Currencies & Economy

### Currency overview
| Currency | Status in Warzone | How earned | What it buys |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gold (Gold Bonds)** | **Removed as a spendable shop currency** in Warzone | Originally purchasable with real money | (Originally) premium cosmetics, ships, boosters. On Warzone launch, remaining Gold balance was converted into **Gold Reward Capsules**; Gold→Silver exchange rate was **doubled** in players' favor. |
| **Silver / "Visk"** | **Primary in-game currency** | Earned from battles, Daily Challenges, Reward Capsules; surplus reduced at conversion | Paint jobs, decals, cockpits, pilot suits, helmets, **and implants/XP boosters** (all now Silver-priced). |
| **Class XP** | Core progression | Per-ship combat performance | Ship upgrade-tree modifications. |
| **Pilot Reputation** | Account rank metric | Aggregated Class XP | Raises Pilot Rank (not spendable). |

### Salvage (in-match resource)
Salvage is picked up during battle (by you or teammates) and **immediately fuels your ship's "Ultra" gauge** (ultimate ability). Three tiers:
| Tier | Rarity |
|---|---|
| **Raw** | Common |
| **Component** | Rare |
| **Prime** | Epic |
Salvage can also be a **reward from Daily Challenges**.

### Microtransactions — Original vs Warzone
- The **original** EVE: Valkyrie was criticized as "pay-for-power": a full-price game where real-money Gold could buy more powerful ships, creating performance gaps vs. paying players.
- **Warzone overhauled the economy:**
  - **Removed Gold** as a shop currency; converted everything purchasable to **Silver earnable through play**.
  - Gold-only cosmetics (cockpits/decals/paint jobs) became Silver-buyable.
  - Implants moved to Silver pricing.
- **Remaining monetization in Warzone:** described as "wholly optional in-game microtransactions" — primarily **Gold Reward Capsules** (premium loot boxes) and possibly cosmetic/booster purchases. **All items remained earnable for free** via gameplay/Reward Capsules. Warzone itself sold for **$29.99 / €29.99**; all content updates (maps/ships/modes) were free post-purchase.
  - **[UNCERTAIN]** Whether real-money purchasing of premium capsules persisted unchanged through Warzone's life, or was further curtailed — official wording emphasizes "everything purchasable can be earned in-game."

## 4. Reward Capsules (Loot Boxes)

- **Reward Capsules** ("Loot Capsules") are earned by playing online battles — **every online battle contributes toward your next capsule**.
- **Contents (random mix):** XP-boosting implants, ship-specific paint jobs, decals, pilot suits, helmets, in-game Silver currency.
- **Gold Reward Capsules** = premium tier (bought via platform stores) with superior loot; all contents still obtainable free.
- Capsules can also be earned from **Scout mode** (trophy "Metal Detective").
- Trophy targets: "Death Metal" (10 capsules from battle), "Sweet Loot" (50 capsules from battle).

## 5. Cosmetics & Customization

All cosmetics are buyable with **Silver (Visk)** or dropped from **Reward Capsules**. Rarity tiers: **Common → Rare → Epic** (paint jobs explicitly span these; "Inner Beauty" trophy = apply an Epic paint job, "Dye Fighter" = apply a Rare paint job).

### Cosmetic types
| Type | Details |
|---|---|
| **Paint Jobs** | "Literally dozens" available. Styles: military camouflage, scorching amber, scarlet flames, verdant greens, vibrant purples. Rare/Epic paint jobs **also change the cockpit interior and HUD look**. |
| **Decals** | Layered on top of the ship; preview & rotate, mix styles + colors. Designs include: skulls, toxic symbols, fangs, defiant fists, smiley faces. |
| **Cockpit interiors** | **Merged with paint jobs** in Warzone (Rare/Epic paints change cockpit + HUD). Styles range from dark/moody low-light to shiny hi-tech to utilitarian. |
| **Pilot suits** | Themed flight suits, e.g. **'Assault'** and **'Stealth'** themes, with color modifications. Male & female variants. |
| **Pilot helmets** | Wide range, Silver-buyable or capsule-earned. |
| **Forum avatars** | Profile/forum avatars (e.g., 14 in the Drifter Pack; platform-colored Founder avatars). |

### Named / notable cosmetics
- **Drifter 'Pulse' Shadow Ship Paint Job (Epic, exclusive)** — Drifter Pack.
- **Drifter 'Voltaic' Pilot Suit (Epic, exclusive)** — Drifter Pack.
- **Drifter 'Voltaic' Pilot Helmet (Epic, exclusive)** — Drifter Pack.
- Founder flight suits coordinated to each platform's Legendary Wraith ship (male & female).
- Platform-colored Founder avatars: **Gold (Oculus), Blue (PSVR), Green (Vive).**

### Legendary Ships (cosmetic + pre-maxed)
Exclusive ships with stats **maxed out of the box** (acquired via Founder's Packs / Quartermaster):
- **Squadron 33 Wraith** — Oculus
- **S4 Wraith** — PlayStation VR
- **MkV Wraith** — HTC Vive

## 6. Leagues / Ranked / Matchmaking & Seasons

### Matchmaking tiers (by Rank)
- Default brackets: **Rank 1–25** pool, then **Rank 26–60** pool once you reach Rank 26.

### Leagues (competitive ladder)
- The **Valkyrie League** was introduced as an **alpha feature** (skill-based, not cumulative stats).
- Reportedly **four leagues** (names/tier structure not publicly specified). **[UNCERTAIN]** — exact league names/divisions never published.
- **Scoring:** based on PvP match score, weighted by individual performance, match balance, time in match, and outcome. **Fighting AI yields less payoff than fighting human opponents.**
- Players could view their position, compare against the community, opt out (casual), and got promotion/demotion notifications.
- **Seasons:** CCP planned to announce the "first true Valkyrie Season" after the alpha trial succeeded; **rewards structure was never publicly detailed.** **[UNCERTAIN]** — unclear whether formal seasons with reward sets ever fully launched before server shutdown.

## 7. Daily Challenges

- New challenge **on login**; a fresh Daily Challenge becomes available **every ~15 hours** after logging in and claiming one. (One source says they refresh daily / expire at day's end — slight conflict. **[UNCERTAIN]**)
- **Rewards:** combination of **XP, Silver (Visk), and Salvage**; some grant multiple rewards.
- Challenges are assigned based on **ship classes you own** (won't get Heavy challenges before Heavy unlocks at rank 3).
- You can **abandon/reroll** an unwanted challenge for an alternative.
- Trackable via the in-game dashboard and (formerly) the website.
- **Named challenge types:** Tourist (play different modes), Slayer, Drone Swatter, Primary Gunner, Mechanic (plus more). Example: "Play 7 battles."
- Trophy: **"Full Deck"** = complete 3 Daily Challenges.
