# EVE: Valkyrie – Warzone — Ships / Fighters (Static Game Data)

**Game:** EVE: Valkyrie (CCP Games, 2016) and its major 2017 free update **EVE: Valkyrie – Warzone**. A multiplayer space-combat shooter set in the EVE Online universe, originally VR-exclusive (Oculus Rift, PlayStation VR, HTC Vive); Warzone (Sept 2017) added flatscreen/non-VR play and reworked the fleet.
**Servers shut down:** 5 August 2022 (game no longer playable online). *(Source: ship pages on the pre-production mirror, see Sources.)*

> **IMPORTANT — two distinct ship rosters.** The game's ship lineup changed substantially between the **original release (2016, "Fighter / Heavy / Support" era)** and the **Warzone update (2017)**, which renamed the Fighter class to **Assault**, added the **Covert** class, and replaced/renamed most ship hulls. This document covers **both** rosters and labels each clearly. The two should not be conflated.
>
> Where a fact comes from the original-release wiki it is marked **[Original 2016]**; where it comes from Warzone-era sources it is marked **[Warzone 2017]**.

## 1. Ship Classes — Overview

### Warzone (2017) — FOUR classes, 13 unique ships
Warzone's fleet comprises **four ship classes and 13 unique ships**, each with unique weapons and a powerful **Ultra** ability. Every ship is available to a pilot from the very first time they enter the Clone Vat — i.e. **all ships are unlocked from the start** in Warzone (progression is per-hull XP that unlocks a modular upgrade tree, not the ships themselves).
*(Sources: PlayStation Blog launch post; evevalkyrie.com /ships; FAQ; "How to upgrade your ships" article.)*

| Class | Role / Design Pillar |
|---|---|
| **Assault** (formerly "Fighter") | Light, fast, weapon-packed mid-range interceptors. Easiest class to pick up; the primary offensive dogfighters that harass and intercept enemy ships. |
| **Heavy** | The "tank" class. Slow and cumbersome but heavily armored/shielded with serious firepower; designed to absorb damage and dish out area-effect punishment, often as part of carrier-assault pushes. |
| **Support** | Team-augmentation ships. They repair/recharge friendly ships or drain/strip enemy shields. Agile, shield-heavy, light armor. |
| **Covert** | **New in Warzone.** Stealth "glass cannon" — a single ship (Shadow) with a cloaking device, low shields/armor, and ambush playstyle built on Drifter technology. |

*(Sources: Steam Assault/Heavy/Support guides; evevalkyrie.com "Get to know the new Assault class", "Warzone: the inside scoop on the Support class", "Revealed at last: the elusive Covert class"; "Designing the Fleet" dev blog.)*

### Original release (2016) — THREE classes
The launch game had **three** classes: **Fighters, Heavies, Support**. Unlock order: you start as a Fighter; the first Heavy and first Support ship are unlocked by playing as a fighter; subsequent Heavy/Support ships are unlocked by earning EXP with any unlocked ship in that category.
*(Source: eve.fandom.com/wiki/EVE:_Valkyrie.)*

## 2. WARZONE (2017) ROSTER — 13 ships

Stats below are pulled from the official EVE: Valkyrie ship pages (retrieved via the live-mirrored `evevalkyriesecure-production-pre-production.azurewebsites.net` host, which serves the same content as the now-offline `evevalkyrie.com/ships/<name>` pages). Numeric values are the in-game display stats. "—" = not stated on the page.

### 2.1 Assault Class (4 ships)

| Ship | Role | Armor | Shields | Base / Max Speed | Primary | Ability 1 | Ability 2 | Ultra |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Wraith** (Mark Six / Mk6) | All-round fighter | 226 | 110 | 200 / 600 | Assault Cannons (150 DPS) | Missile Launcher (25 dmg) | ECM (15s CD / 3s dur) | **Mjolnir Missile** |
| **Jackal** | Brawler (close-range) | 210 | 168 | 140 / 600 | Ripper (56 DPS) | Ripper Alt (100 DPS) | Overshield (60s CD / 30s dur) | **Matter Injector** |
| **Strix** | Sniper (long-range) | 112 | 224 | 200 / 500 | Pulse Laser (60 DPS) | Missile Pod (15 dmg) | Dash (30s CD / 2s dur) | **Plasma Vent** |
| **Yurei** | Interceptor | 110 | 112 | 212 / 750 | Pulse Cannons (150 DPS) | Trine Missiles (20 dmg) | ECM (15s CD / 3s dur) | **Mayhem** |

- **Wraith** — "Refined and finessed over many generations, the Wraith Mark Six has evolved alongside the pilots who fly its iconic frame." The default starter / first ship; fast, nimble, deadly at medium range. Ultra **Mjolnir** = a one-shot super missile that penetrates shields/armor for catastrophic damage.
- **Jackal** — "Bold and brutal… twin-shot blast cannons excel at close-range combat." Thickened hull. Ultra **Matter Injector** = unlimited capacitor/boost for a short period (great for carrier attacks).
- **Strix** — Precision sharpshooter. Ultra **Plasma Vent** = unlimited shots with no overheat penalty for a short time. Has a forward **Dash** for evasion. Missile Pod fires up to ~7 splash missiles.
- **Yurei** — Sustained mid-range damage via dual Pulse Cannons. Ultra **Mayhem** = increased weapons damage. Highest top speed in the Assault class (750).

*(Sources: evevalkyrie ship pages /wraith /jackal /strix /yurei; Steam "Assault Ship Guide" id=1134260537; PCGamesN class guide.)*

### 2.2 Heavy Class (4 ships)

| Ship | Role | Armor | Shields | Base / Max Speed | Primary | Ability 1 | Ability 2 | Ultra |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Scarab** | Tank | 336 | 336 | 200 / 400 | Flak Cannons (100 DPS) | Microwarp Drive (15s/4s) | EMS (15s/6s) | **Void Storm** |
| **Vulcan** | Brute | 400 | 400 | 200 / 400 | Laser Gatlings (100 DPS) | Microwarp Drive (15s/4s) | Self Repair (45s/5s) | **Cocoon** |
| **Corvus** | Disruptor | 224 | 448 | 200 / 500 | Tri Flak Cannon (100 DPS) | Sonar (30s CD / 10s detect) | EMS (15s/6s) | **Silence** |
| **Hydra** | Gunner | 448 | 224 | 200 / 500 | Flak Cannon (46 DPS) | Rail Gun / Rail Cannon (54 DPS) | ECM (15s/3s) | **Spectre Bond** |

- **Scarab** — Heavily plated "thick compressed armor" tank; area-effect Flak. Ultra **Void Storm** stuns nearby enemies.
- **Vulcan** — "A flying barrage of deadly neon beams"; spinning four-barrel Laser Gatling. Highest armor+shields in the game (400/400). Ultra **Cocoon** = temporary invulnerability; can Self Repair.
- **Corvus** — "The great disruptor"; uses **Sonar** to reveal cloaked ships (counter to the Shadow). Ultra **Silence** stuns ships within a pulse radius.
- **Hydra** — Dual-weapon "gunner": Flak Cannon + armor-piercing Rail Gun. Ultra **Spectre Bond / Spectre's Bond** = brief damage immunity.

*(Sources: evevalkyrie ship pages /scarab /vulcan /corvus /hydra; Steam "Heavy Ship Guide" id=1134266763; Heavy-class YouTube tutorials.)*

### 2.3 Support Class (4 ships)

| Ship | Role | Armor | Shields | Base / Max Speed | Primary | Ability 1 | Ability 2 | Ultra |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Banshee** | Engineer | 124 | 228 | 300 / 600 | Phaser (75 DPS) | Drain Beam (10/s drain) | Spiderbots (10s CD; 30 heal/s, 30 dmg/s) | **Fate's Call** |
| **Nagi** | Medic (team support) | 124 | 228 | 300 / 600 | Twin Linked Carbine (26 DPS) | Heal Beam / Repair Beam (15/s) | Self Repair (45s/5s) | **Radiant Burst** |
| **Medusa** | Defender | 228 | 124 | 300 / 600 | Pulse Cannon (150 DPS) | Overshield Beam (15s CD) | EMS (15s/6s) | **Death's Grasp** |
| **Kirin** | Saboteur | 228 | 124 | 300 / 600 | Shard Rifle (75 DPS) | Capacitor Restore Beam (10/s) | HUD Scrambler (30s/5s) | **Dragon's Thorn** |

- **Banshee** — The default Support ship. Phaser primary; Drain Beam strips enemy shields; Spiderbots heal allies / damage enemies. Ultra **Fate's Call** shields nearby teammates.
- **Nagi** — Healing/repair "wingman." Repair/Heal Beam restores ally shields & armor. Ultra **Radiant Burst** repairs hulls of all nearby teammates.
- **Medusa** — Shield-focused defender. Overshield Beam adds temporary shields to allies; EMS Pulse Cannon. Ultra **Death's Grasp** strips nearby enemy shields.
- **Kirin** — Disruptive "saboteur." Shard Rifle (sticky delayed-detonation projectiles); Capacitor Restore Beam recharges teammate energy; HUD Scrambler confuses enemies. Ultra **Dragon's Thorn** = sticky explosive bomb.

*(Sources: evevalkyrie ship pages /banshee /nagi /medusa /kirin; Steam "Support Ship Guide" id=1138771785; "Warzone: the inside scoop on the Support class".)*

### 2.4 Covert Class (1 ship)

| Ship | Role | Armor | Shields | Base / Max Speed | Primary | Ability 1 | Ability 2 | Ultra |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Shadow** | Ambush / assassin | 140 | 60 | 400 / 600 | Plasma Beam (33 DPS) | Mark Target (30s CD / 10s dur) | Cloak (15s CD / 30s dur) | **Violent Whisper** |

- **Shadow** — The only Covert ship. Built on **Drifter technology**; a stealth glass cannon (lowest armor/shields in the game). **Cloak** turns it invisible for 30 seconds; **Mark Target** flags an enemy so the Shadow and teammates do bonus damage. Plasma Beam fires 4-shot bursts or one charged heavy blast. Ultra **Violent Whisper** = a massive nova bomb you drop then flee from before it detonates. Hard-countered by the Corvus's Sonar.

*(Sources: evevalkyrie ship page /shadow; "Revealed at last: the elusive Covert class"; PCGamesN Covert article; PC Gamer covert-ship article; Covert-class YouTube tutorial.)*

## 4. Weapons & Equipment Glossary (original-release definitions, largely carry over)
- **Gatling Guns** — very high rate of fire, low per-shot damage; good vs shields & hulls.
- **Auto Cannons** — slower than gatlings, higher per-shot; good vs shields & hulls.
- **Pulse Cannons** — slower-firing; good vs shields, weak vs hull.
- **Magcannons** — partial aim-assist/homing, high per-shot, short bursts.
- **Flak Cannons** — proximity-fused AoE; forward or head-tracked; good vs armor & shields.
- **Tri-Flak Cannons** — higher-damage flak, head-tracked, Heavy-only.
- **Phasers** — medium-range charge-up beams with strong aim-assist; low DPS; vs shields & hulls.
- **Missiles / Missile Pods / Mortar (Maelstrom-only)** — head-tracked; Mortar = slow, low ROF, extreme direct-hit damage with AoE cluster explosions.
- **Equipment/utility:** Countermeasures (ECM/chaff), EMS (blocks missiles, can apply EMP status), EMP Mine, Mine, Spiderbots, Microwarp Drive, Self Repair, Buff Beam (heal allies / drain enemies).

*(Source: eve.fandom.com/wiki/EVE:_Valkyrie.)*

> Note: §4 mentions a **Maelstrom** (Mortar) and references the **Phantom** (Missile Pods). The Maelstrom appears in the weapons glossary and in some Warzone "Heavies" tactics articles but did **not** confirm as one of the 13 Warzone hulls — see Gaps.

## 5. Faction / Manufacturer / Lore Tie-ins

- The game is set in the **EVE Online** universe; the playable pilots are the **Valkyrie** mercenary clones (capsuleer-derived). Many ship and NPC names are reused EVE Online hull/lore names.
- **Shadow (Covert)** is explicitly built from **Drifter technology** — the Drifters being a hostile NPC faction in EVE Online lore. *(evevalkyrie /shadow; Covert reveal article.)*
- Several **NPC ship names** (Merlin, Kestrel, Hookbill, Blackbird, Dragonfly) are **Caldari State** frigate hulls in EVE Online; **Templar** ties to the Amarr-flavored "Valkyrie/Templar" clone-soldier lore from CCP's earlier *DUST 514* / EVE lore. *(eve.fandom wiki + EVE Online lore cross-reference.)*
- **Per-ship in-universe manufacturer/faction attributions were NOT found.** The Warzone ship pages give role + flavor lore but do **not** assign a specific manufacturer corporation or empire faction (Amarr/Caldari/Gallente/Minmatar) to each of the 13 hulls. The "Designing the Fleet" dev blog frames ships by *class design pillars and gameplay identity*, not by manufacturer faction. See Gaps.

## 6. Starter vs Unlockable Ships

- **Warzone (2017):** *All 13 ships are available from the start* (first Clone Vat entry). There is no ship-unlock gate; progression is per-hull XP feeding a 15-node modular upgrade tree (4 upgrade classes: Attack, Defense, Tech, Mobility; one active upgrade per class). Default first ships shown to new pilots are **Wraith** (Assault), **Spectre** (the original base Heavy — but in Warzone the base Heavy is **Scarab**), and **Banshee** (Support). *(PlayStation Blog; FAQ; "How to upgrade your ships"; one search snippet named Wraith/Spectre/Banshee as the 3 base ships — note Spectre is the **original** base Heavy and may reflect pre-Warzone info; see Gaps.)*
- **Original release (2016):** Start as a **Wraith** (Fighter). First Heavy (**Spectre**) and first Support (**Banshee**) unlock through fighter play; further Heavy/Support hulls unlock via category EXP. *(eve.fandom wiki.)*

## 8. Cosmetics / Skin Variants
- Ships can be customized in the **Hangar** with **paint jobs and decals**, purchasable with **Silver (VISK)** or earned from the **Reward Capsule** system; the system also includes pilot helmets and flight suits. *(evevalkyrie "Ships, Suits and Avatars – Valkyrie's Cool Collectibles".)*

## 9. Gaps / Could Not Confirm
1. **Per-ship manufacturer/faction.** No source assigns each of the 13 Warzone hulls (or original hulls) to a specific EVE Online manufacturer corporation or empire (Amarr/Caldari/Gallente/Minmatar). Only the Shadow's "Drifter technology" origin is explicit.
2. **Original→Warzone hull mapping.** Whether Warzone hulls are renames of 2016 hulls or new designs is not definitively documented. Likely changes (inferred, not confirmed): Fighter→Assault class rename; original Heavies (Spectre, Rhino, Goliath, Cyclone, Storm) appear largely replaced by Warzone Heavies (Scarab, Vulcan, Corvus, Hydra). Note "Spectre" survives only as the name of Hydra's Ultra ("Spectre Bond") in Warzone.
3. **Maelstrom (Mortar) and Phantom in Warzone.** The Maelstrom (Mortar weapon) and Phantom (Missile Pods) appear in the weapons glossary / some tactics articles, but neither is among the confirmed 13 Warzone hulls. Their Warzone status is unconfirmed (possibly cut, possibly NPC/event).
4. **Base Heavy in Warzone:** one search snippet listed Wraith/Spectre/Banshee as the three "base" ships, but Spectre is the *original* base Heavy; in Warzone the Heavy lineup is Scarab/Vulcan/Corvus/Hydra. The exact "default highlighted" Heavy in Warzone's UI is unconfirmed (likely Scarab as "Tank"/base).
5. **Difficulty ratings.** Each Warzone ship page lists a "Difficulty" field, but the numeric/visual value was not captured (only "Standard" for Kirin was returned).
6. **Original-release stats vs Warzone stats** use different scales (X/10 bars in 2016 vs raw values like armor 226 in Warzone) and are not directly comparable.
7. **Bounty, Infected, Firbolg, several NPC ships** — no stats available (wiki lists "Unknown").
8. **Full named cosmetic/skin catalogue per ship** — not located beyond the Founder Legendary Wraiths.
